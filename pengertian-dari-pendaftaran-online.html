Past Continuous:

The present continuous, also called the present progressive or present imperfect, is a verb form used in modern English that combines the present tense with the continuous aspect.

(+) Positive:

• S + Was/Were + Verb + -ing

• Examples:

- I was reading a book called The Starless Sea.

- He was making flowers out of newspapers.

- They were doing a math test.

(-) Negative:

• S + Wasn't/Weren't + Verb + -ing

• Examples:

- You weren't loud enough.

- He wasn't watching cartoons in his room.

- She wasn't doing her homework.

(?) Interrogative:

• Was/Were + S + Verb + -ing + ?

• Examples:

- Was I dreaming?

- Were you going somewhere?

- Was it real?
